I am currently at the midpoint of my internship journey with the VLED Lab IIT Ropar, and if I had to sum up my experience so far, it would be this: lectures teach you the ideal scenario, but practical application teaches you the messy reality. When we started with the MERN stack courses and , everything felt straightforward. When an instructor writes code, the logic flows perfectly, and there are no unexpected crashes. But the moment I transitioned from passively watching lectures to actually building things, I made a lot of mistakes. And honestly, those mistakes have been my biggest teachers over these past few weeks. The most significant shift for me so far has been taking a purely practical approach. When you start working on an actual web application, you suddenly realize how many crucial details are completely ignored during theoretical learning. In lectures, we focus heavily on the core logic. But in practice, I discovered that the "small things"—like setting up a proper development environment, managing folder structures meticulously, configuring database connections, and dealing with dependencies—are what actually hold a project together. Working through the case studies forced me to face these blind spots. It is frustrating when a minor configuration error breaks the entire app, but debugging it has taught me more than any tutorial ever could.

 More importantly, this entire process greatly provides a research-oriented approach. It doesn't just train you to write syntax; it deepens your thoughtfulness as a person. You stop accepting things at face value and start deeply investigating the 'why' behind every bug and architecture choice.

 As I stand at this halfway mark, I realize this internship isn't just teaching me how to code; it’s changing my mindset. The journey is only half done, and I am actually looking forward to the messy, real-world challenges the rest of this internship will bring. VLED Labs Indian Institute of Technology, Ropar Sudarshan Iyengar
